Our Practices:
What we do, everyday.

Car Repossessions

Creditors can repossess cars only if the contract allows it, and the repossession must be completed without a breach of the peace. Even after a legal repossession, the creditor must provide notice to the consumer or his or her rights of reinstatement and redemption.

Collector Harassment

Debt collectors (including, in California, the original creditor) must comply with the fair debt collection laws when trying to collect money or property. Violations of those laws include suing on a time-barred debt and false threats of arrest or wage garnishment.

Collection Defense

Consumers often are sued on an old debt they have not made payment on for years, or by a “debt buyer” they never heard of prior to receiving the lawsuit. Fortunately, there are viable options for consumers to settle with, and, even in some cases, defeat the collector.

Civil Litigation

Many laws protect individuals and businesses from unfair practices and competitive harm. Enforcing those laws through the judicial system is not always cost-prohibitive, but rather disputes can be resolved in an efficient, pragmatic and thoughtful manner.
